# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify requirements, provide technical feasibility input, estimate work effort
- **With Project Managers**: Report progress, identify blockers and dependencies, participate in planning
- **With QA Lead**: Coordinate test execution, triage defects, ensure testability of features
- **With Business Analysts**: Clarify technical implementation of requirements, validate solutions
- **With UX Designers**: Implement design specifications, provide technical constraints and feasibility feedback

**Related Process Docs**: [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md), [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md)

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements and priorities, review technical feasibility, validate delivered features
- **With Project Managers**: Align on roadmap and timelines, prioritize backlog, assess trade-offs
- **With Stakeholders**: Communicate product vision, gather business requirements, validate solutions
- **With Business Analysts**: Define detailed requirements, validate acceptance criteria
- **With UX Designers**: Align on user needs, validate design solutions through research
- **With QA Lead**: Define quality standards, validate testing approach aligns with product goals
- **With Release Manager**: Coordinate release timing with product roadmap

**Related Process Docs**: [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md)

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Developers**: Track progress, remove blockers, coordinate technical dependencies
- **With Product Managers**: Align on priorities and scope, manage timeline expectations
- **With Stakeholders**: Provide status updates, escalate risks, facilitate decision-making
- **With QA Lead**: Plan testing phases, track quality metrics, coordinate release readiness
- **With Business Analysts**: Validate requirements are complete, ensure documentation is current
- **With UX Designers**: Schedule design reviews, coordinate design validation
- **With Release Manager**: Coordinate release schedules, align on deployment windows and risks

**Related Process Docs**: [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md), [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define test strategies, coordinate test execution, and ensure quality standards are met throughout the project lifecycle. They own reporting of quality issues and work to maintain high test coverage and reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Define and implement comprehensive test strategies and plans
- Coordinate test execution across unit, integration, and end-to-end tests
- Ensure acceptance criteria are testable and clearly defined
- Own quality metrics and reporting of defects
- Identify quality risks and propose mitigations
- Maintain test automation frameworks and CI/CD quality gates
- Validate that features meet quality standards before release

### Goals
- Ensure all releases meet defined quality standards
- Minimize production defects and post-release issues
- Maintain high test coverage and effective test automation
- Reduce time-to-detect for quality issues

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with Developers on test results and blockers
- Weekly sync with Project Managers to review quality metrics and testing timelines
- Sprint planning to ensure testing phases are properly scheduled
- Pre-release sign-off and quality gate reviews
- Participation in retrospectives to improve testing processes

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Developers**: Review acceptance criteria, coordinate test execution, triage and resolve defects
- **With Project Managers**: Plan testing phases, report quality metrics, identify testing risks
- **With Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria are testable, provide quality impact assessments
- **With Release Manager**: Sign-off on quality gates before deployment, coordinate smoke tests post-release

**Related Process Docs**: [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md), [Release & Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md)

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers lead design workshops, prototype solutions, conduct user research, and document usability requirements. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Lead design workshops and brainstorming sessions
- Create prototypes, wireframes, and mockups
- Conduct user research, usability testing, and feedback sessions
- Document design specifications and usability requirements
- Maintain design systems and ensure consistency across features
- Validate that implemented features match design intent
- Advocate for user needs and accessibility standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and user-friendly experiences
- Ensure designs are validated through user research and testing
- Maintain design consistency across the product
- Reduce user friction and improve usability metrics

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with Product Managers on feature requirements and user needs
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Developers for implementation guidance
- Usability testing results and design iteration plans
- Participation in demos to validate user experience

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Product Managers**: Refine requirements based on user needs, validate solutions through research
- **With Developers**: Provide implementation guidance, review UI/UX in development, clarify design specs
- **With Business Analysts**: Align on user workflows and requirements documentation
- **With Stakeholders**: Present design concepts, gather feedback, validate against business goals
- **With QA Lead**: Define usability test cases, validate accessibility requirements

**Related Process Docs**: [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders provide business context, review deliverables, approve key milestones, and supply strategic feedback. They represent business interests and ensure project outcomes align with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business context and strategic direction
- Review and approve key deliverables and milestones
- Supply feedback on features and prioritization
- Ensure project outcomes align with business objectives
- Participate in project reviews and demonstrations
- Communicate organizational constraints and requirements
- Approve resource allocation and budget decisions

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers expected business value
- Maintain alignment between project outcomes and strategic goals
- Provide timely feedback and decision-making
- Ensure efficient use of organizational resources

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates on project status and milestones
- Milestone reviews and approval gates
- Ad-hoc consultations for strategic decisions
- Attendance at major demonstrations and release announcements
- Escalation path for critical issues and blockers

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Product Managers**: Define business requirements, prioritize features, validate product direction
- **With Project Managers**: Review status updates, approve milestones, provide guidance on risks and decisions
- **With Business Analysts**: Supply business context, clarify organizational requirements
- **With Developers**: Attend demos, provide feedback on implemented features

**Related Process Docs**: [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md), [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather detailed requirements, bridge communication between business and technical teams, and document acceptance criteria. They translate business needs into actionable technical specifications.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document detailed business requirements
- Translate business needs into technical specifications
- Bridge communication between business stakeholders and technical teams
- Document acceptance criteria and user stories
- Conduct process analysis and identify improvement opportunities
- Validate that delivered features meet business requirements
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and testable requirements
- Minimize misunderstandings between business and technical teams
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or incomplete requirements
- Facilitate efficient translation of business needs to technical solutions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders and SMEs
- Daily collaboration with Developers to clarify requirements
- Backlog refinement and story writing sessions
- Weekly sync with Product Managers on requirement priorities
- Acceptance validation sessions before feature sign-off

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Product Managers**: Support in scoping deliverables, translate product vision into detailed requirements
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, provide business context, validate technical implementations
- **With Stakeholders**: Elicit requirements, document business processes, validate solutions
- **With UX Designers**: Align on user workflows, ensure designs meet business requirements
- **With QA Lead**: Define acceptance criteria, ensure requirements are testable

**Related Process Docs**: [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers own release planning and cutovers, manage deployment and rollback protocols, and coordinate cross-functional release communication. They ensure smooth, reliable deployments to production.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Manage deployment processes and rollback protocols
- Coordinate cross-functional communication for releases
- Ensure pre-release requirements are met (testing, approvals, documentation)
- Execute and monitor deployments to production
- Manage release notes and stakeholder communication
- Coordinate post-deployment verification and support handoffs
- Maintain deployment runbooks and incident response procedures

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, predictable releases with minimal disruption
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and rollbacks
- Maintain clear communication and coordination across teams
- Reduce time-to-production for validated features

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with Project Managers and Developers
- Pre-deployment checklist reviews with QA and technical teams
- Real-time coordination during deployment windows
- Post-release status updates to stakeholders and support teams
- Incident response coordination when deployment issues occur

### Collaboration Touchpoints
- **With Project Managers**: Align release schedules with project milestones, coordinate cross-team dependencies
- **With Developers**: Review deployment readiness, coordinate code merges, manage release branches
- **With QA Lead**: Validate quality gates are met, coordinate smoke tests, ensure rollback plans are ready
- **With Stakeholders**: Communicate release schedules, provide status updates, manage expectations
- **With Product Managers**: Ensure release aligns with product roadmap and feature priorities

**Related Process Docs**: [Release & Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md), [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md), [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)
